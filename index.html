About Me

I’ve always been fascinated by the intersection of biology and technology, especially how AI can be used to model and support biological systems.

This June, I graduated with honors from Cal Poly with a B.S. in Biological Sciences, concentrating in Cellular and Molecular Biology, and minors in Computer Science and Bioinformatics. Over the past four years, I’ve had the opportunity to delve into research that has fueled my curiosity in bioinformatics and the role AI can play in solving real biological problems.

Along the way, I’ve contributed to projects such as:

  • Building a modular and reproducible RNA-seq analysis pipeline with real-time parameter tuning and interactive visualizations
  • Applying deep learning to denoise fluorescence microscopy images
  • Evaluating model predictions in a supervised anemia detection task to assess biological relevance and potential bias
  • Developing methods for entity resolution in a knowledge-graph-powered application for chrnoic lower back pain (CLBP)
  • Exploring connections between chemical spills and cancer rates using machine learning and public health data
  • Analyzing methods for classifying carbonic anhydrase proteins using clustering and MSTA
  • Investigating the use of real-world evidence (RWE) in FDA drug approvals

Currently, I am applying to graduate programs, where I hope to continue tackling complex problems in bioinformatics using data and computation to better understand biology and improve health outcomes.
